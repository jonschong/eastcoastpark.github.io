East Coast Park

A heaven filled with fun and adventure!

East Coast Park is one of Singapore's most treasured urban getaways, offering a exciting diversity of recreational, sporting and dining activities. With the theme Recreation for All, the park has an activity for everyone.

    Things To Do

  • Wakeboarding

    • Looking for a water sport to expend the endless energy your children have? Want a fun half-day out as a family while imagining you are on the set of Hawaii Five-O? Try wakeboarding at the Singapore Wake Park in East Coast Park.

    Singapore Wake Park is the countrys only cable -ski park built in a lagoon at Singapore East Coast Park. It ensures a safe environment for both beginners and experienced riders.

    Opening Hours:
    10am - 9pm (Mon - Fri)
    9am - 8pm (Sat - Sun)

    For rates and booking, click here.

  • Changi Jurrasic Mile

    • Jurassic Mile is Singapore newest and largest permanent outdoor display of life-sized dinosaurs. More than 20 different pre-historic creatures from nine different species stretch over 1-kilometre of the cycling and jogging path. Expect to see carnivorous and herbivorous species, defensive dinosaurs, as well as those that fly. The tallest dinosaur stands at almost 5 meters high!

    From a towering Tyrannosaurus Rex to gigantic dinosaur eggs, visitors will be wowed by the lifelike dinosaur exhibits at the Changi Jurassic Mile, making it a perfect outdoor weekend destination for families and friends.

    Opening Hours:
    10am - 9pm (Mon - Fri)
    9am - 8pm (Sat - Sun)

    For rates and booking, click here.

  • Fishing

    • Fishing is one of the many ways of spending an afternoon by the beach or in the park. Did you know that one of Singapore most beloved parks also houses a popular fishing location? East Coast park offers both shallow and deep water fishing to capture a variety of fishes. Here are some popular fishing spots!

    Bedok Jetty
    You can go for the challenge of shallow water fishing just off the coast, or head further away to the deeper ends for more intense angling.

    Changi Beach
    This stretch of beach is known to produce big stingrays too like this Leopard Ray.

    Breakwaters
    There are many breakwaters along the 15km stretch of East Coast Park. These structures serve as good hiding place for predators to ambush their prey.

    Car/Taxi

    The easiest way to get there is by driving and parking at one of their 10 car parks lined along the length of the park. Car park locations and parking rates can be found here

    Public Transport

    TFor those taking public transport, Bedok MRT is the nearest station to the park. From there, Buses 31, 137, 155 or 196 will take you to the middle of the park, where attractions like Bedok Jetty and Singapore Wake Park lie.
